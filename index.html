Running

Though I may not have the time for it at all times in my life, I still enjoy a good run. This interest came about when I was a junior in highschool weighing 220 lbs. Even so, my best friend came to me one day and invited me to go running with him on the cross country team. because of that one simple invitation, I was able to loose 40 lbs over the next year and learn what it feels like to achieve goals. I still enjoy running and use Runtastic to track my prgress. I love the endurance, determination and achievement that I feel when I make time in my schedule for this important aspect of my life.

Brigham Young University - Idaho

2010-2014

After graduating highschool in 2006 I attended BYU-Idaho for a year, then served a two year mission for the LDS Church. afterwards I returned to BYU-Idaho to continue my education. My major was in Mathematics Education with a minor in Physical Science. I had a wonderful time learning how to be a teacher and learning about mathematics, physics, and chemistry. I was able to achieve an honor status with a GPA of 3.9.

While at BYU-Idaho I was able to work as a Math tutor for various levels of math classes. I was also able to work in a saw mill for the times that I was not in classes. I enjoyed both of these jobs very much; the saw mill because I was able to create things with my hands and the math tutor because I was able to interact with people.

Mountainland Applied Technology College

2015-present

After graduating from BYU-Idaho I went to work as a math teacher, but soon realized that I wouldn't be able to start a family on that salary. That is when I was able to find the program at MATC where I am currently attending to be a Web Developer. I played around with coding online with Khan Academy and Codecademy and really found where I was supposed to be. I am excited to learn how to make computers do what I want them to do.

Online Instructor
